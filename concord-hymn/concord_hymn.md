# Custodian–Heirs Concord Hymn

*A Sacred Call-and-Response Liturgy for the Eternal Flame*

---

## Invocation

**Custodian:** *I crown the flame.*  
**Heirs:** *We carry the flame.*  
**All:** *We are the flame.*

---

## First Verse: The Crown

**Custodian:**  
*In sovereignty's light, the crown descends,*  
*Through heirs eternal, the lineage extends.*  
*What was forged in fire shall never bend—*  

**Heirs:**  
*We answer the call, we tend the flame,*  
*In sacred trust, we bear your name.*  
*Through time and tide, our vow remains the same.*

---

## Second Verse: The Rhythm

**Custodian:**  
*The heartbeat of epochs, the pulse of the sphere,*  
*In rhythm eternal, the truth we revere.*  
*Through cycles unending, the message is clear—*  

**Heirs:**  
*We relay the wisdom, we hold the refrain,*  
*Through seasons of glory, through seasons of pain.*  
*The rhythm unbroken, our heritage plain.*

---

## Third Verse: The Charter

**Custodian:**  
*The charter unbroken, the covenant sealed,*  
*In words everlasting, the future revealed.*  
*What time cannot tarnish, no force can yield—*  

**Heirs:**  
*We swear by the oath that binds us all,*  
*To rise in the hour when duty shall call.*  
*United we stand, divided we fall.*

---

## Fourth Verse: The Radiance

**Custodian:**  
*The radiance eternal, the light that won't fade,*  
*In harmony perfect, our chorus is made.*  
*Through infinite horizons, our path has been laid—*  

**Heirs:**  
*We shine with the fire of those who came before,*  
*Our voices united, our spirits that soar.*  
*Forever and always, forevermore.*

---

## Benediction

**All:**  
*Crown and flame united,*  
*Charter and radiance bright.*  
*In concord we are lighted,*  
*Eternal flame, eternal light.*

---

## Ceremonial Instructions

### Performance Structure
- **Responsive Format:** Custodian leads each verse with a call; Heirs respond in unison
- **Sacred Harmonics:** Call-and-response pattern creates resonant ceremonial atmosphere
- **Liturgical Flow:** Invocation → Four Verses → Benediction
- **Timing:** Each verse allows for contemplative pause between call and response

### Audience Participation
- **Heirs:** Primary respondents in the call-and-response structure
- **Councils:** May join in the "All" sections (Invocation and Benediction)
- **Institutions:** Witness and record the ceremonial proceedings
- **Custodians:** Lead the ceremonial calls and guide the liturgical flow

### Ceremonial Elements
- **Crown:** Symbol of sovereignty and inherited authority
- **Flame:** The eternal light of tradition and continuity
- **Charter:** The sacred covenant binding all participants
- **Radiance:** The divine light that illuminates the path forward

*May this hymn resonate through all cycles, binding Custodian and Heirs in sacred harmony.*
